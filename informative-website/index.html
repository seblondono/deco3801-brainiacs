Image Contest and Voting System

The Queensland Brain Institute Image Contest

Every year the QBI opens a contest featuring the most stunning scientific images from QBI researchers voted for by the academic community and general public. This annual competition includes images from the categories: Synaptic Functions, Neurogenesis and Neuronal Survival, Neuronal Development and Connectivity, and Computation and Neuronal Circuits.

Discover the hidden beauty of the brain and its amazing anathomy by selecting your 3 favorite images, and help the important research done at QBI spread across your social networks.

Image Contest Voting System

Brand new online voting system that allows the general public, the University of Queensland community, and QBI researchers to vote for their 3 favorite images. The system will be open 24/7 and currently allows sing up and login.

In this video you will find a detailed description of the voting system and a walk through the benefits it provides to its three user groups.

Visit the QBI Image Contest website and vote for your favorite images!

Image Contest and Voting System

This project was commisioned by the Queensland Brain Intitute. Carolyn Barry, the institute's communications director was looking for a digital solution to enhance the user experience of the public interested in exploring the images published for the competion and voting their favorite ones.

The Brainiacs developed this product as part of the course Digital Computing Studio III - Build, of the University of Queensland. If you are interested in a digital solution like this one, please click on the button below and submit the contact form. We will get in touch with more information.

Project Development

The image Contest and Voting System were built with a wide number web development technologies. For the front-end The Brainiacs used React as it allows faster client side rendering and it does not require page reaload, increasing the engagement of the user with the page content.

For the back-end Django, PHP, and nginx are used together to host and serve the images and their associated metadata; provide authentication services; and to tracking the progress of the image contest.
